# Team Profile: Saudi Arabia

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Giorgios Donis
- **Key Players**: Mohammed Al Owais, Nawaf Al Aqidi, Ahmed Al Kassar, Abdulelah Al Amri, Saud Abdulhamid, Ali Lajami, Hassan Al Tambakti, Hassan Kadesh, Moteb Al Harbi, Ali Majrashi, Jehad Thakri, Nawaf Boushal, Salem Al Dawsari, Abdullah Al Khaibari, Nasser Al Dawsari, Ayman Yahya, Musab Al Juwayr, Mohammed Kanno, Ziyad Al-Johani, Mohammed Abu Al Shamat, Alaa Al Hejji, Sultan Mandash, Saleh Al Shehri, Abdullah Al-Hamdan, Khalid Al Ghannam, Firas Al Brikan
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Saudi Arabia implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Mohammed Al Owais to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Giorgios Donis demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Saudi Arabia excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

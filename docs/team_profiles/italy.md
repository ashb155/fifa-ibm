# Team Profile: Italy

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Luciano Spalletti
- **Key Players**: Nicolò Barella, Federico Chiesa, Gianluigi Donnarumma, Alessandro Bastoni
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Proactive possession play prioritizing attacking over traditional Catenaccio.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Italy implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Nicolò Barella to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Luciano Spalletti demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Italy excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

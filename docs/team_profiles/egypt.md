# Team Profile: Egypt

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Hossam Hassan
- **Key Players**: Mohamed El Shenawy, Mohamed Alaa, Oufa Shobeir, El Mahdi Soliman, Karim Hafez, Mohamed Hany, Yasser Ibrahim, Ahmed El Fotouh, Mohamed Abdelmonem, Rami Rabia, Hossam Abdelmaguid, Tarek Alaa, Zizo, Nabil Dunga, Hamdy Fathy, Trezeguet, Emam Ashour, Mohanad Lashin, Marwan Attia, Mahmoud Saber, Mostafa Ziko, Mohamed Salah, Omar Marmoush, Haissem Hassan, Ibrahim Adel, Hamza Abdelkarim
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Egypt implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Mohamed El Shenawy to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Hossam Hassan demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Egypt excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

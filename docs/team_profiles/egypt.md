# Team Profile: Egypt

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Hossam Hassan
- **Key Players**: Mohamed Salah, Mostafa Mohamed, Omar Marmoush, Mohamed Elneny
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Structured and cautious, funneling all attacking responsibility through their right-sided superstar.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Egypt implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Mohamed Salah to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Hossam Hassan demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Egypt excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

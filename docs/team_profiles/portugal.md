# Team Profile: Portugal

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Roberto Martínez
- **Key Players**: José Sá, Rui Silva, Diogo Costa, João Cancelo, Rúben Dias, Nélson Semedo, Diogo Dalot, Matheus Nunes, Nuno Mendes, Gonçalo Inácio, Tomas Araujo, Renato Veiga, Bernardo Silva, Rúben Neves, Bruno Fernandes, Gonçalo Guedes, Samuel Almeida Costa, Vitinha, Joao Neves, Cristiano Ronaldo, Pedro Neto, Rafael Leão, Trincão, João Félix, Gonçalo Ramos, Francisco Conceição
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Portugal implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for José Sá to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Roberto Martínez demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Portugal excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

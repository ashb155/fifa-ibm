# Team Profile: Bosnia and Herzegovina

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Sergej Barbarez
- **Key Players**: Martin Zlomislić, Nikola Vasilj, Mladen Jurkas, Sead Kolašinac, Dennis Hadžikadunić, Nihad Mujakić, Nikola Katić, Stjepan Radeljić, Amar Dedić, Tarik Muharemović, Arjan Malic, Dženis Burnić, Ivan Šunjić, Amir Hadžiahmetović, Armin Gigovic, Ivan Bašić, Benjamin Tahirovic, Ermin Mahmic, Amar Memić, Edin Džeko, Jovo Lukić, Ermedin Demirovic, Haris Tabaković, Samed Bazdar, Esmir Bajraktarevic, Kerim Alajbegović
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Bosnia and Herzegovina implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Martin Zlomislić to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Sergej Barbarez demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Bosnia and Herzegovina excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

# Team Profile: Mexico

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Javier Aguirre
- **Key Players**: Guillermo Ochoa, Carlos Acevedo, José Rangel, Jesús Gallardo, César Montes, Johan Vásquez, Jorge Sánchez, Israel Reyes, Mateo Chávez, Edson Álvarez, Orbelín Pineda, Luis Chávez, Luis Romo, Erik Lira, Álvaro Fidalgo, Brian Gutiérrez, Obed Vargas, Gilberto Mora, Raúl Jiménez, Julián Quiñones, Roberto Alvarado, Santiago Giménez, Alexis Vega, Guillermo Martínez, César Huerta, Armando González
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Mexico implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Guillermo Ochoa to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Javier Aguirre demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Mexico excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

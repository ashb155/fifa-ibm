# Team Profile: Mexico

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Jaime Lozano
- **Key Players**: Edson Álvarez, Santiago Giménez, Hirving Lozano, César Montes
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Possession-oriented with aggressive overlapping fullbacks.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Mexico implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Edson Álvarez to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Jaime Lozano demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Mexico excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

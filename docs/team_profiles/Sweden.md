# Team Profile: Sweden

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Graham Potter
- **Key Players**: Kristoffer Nordfeldt, Viktor Johansson, Jacob Widell Zetterström, Victor Nilsson-Lindelöf, Carl Starfelt, Gabriel Gudmundsson, Hjalmar Ekdal, Eric Smith, Daniel Svensson, Gustaf Lagerbielke, Isak Hien, Herman Johansson, Elliot Stroud, Ken Sema, Mattias Svanberg, Jesper Karlström, Yasin Ayari, Besfort Zeneli, Taha Ali, Lucas Bergvall, Alexander Isak, Viktor Gyökeres, Gustaf Nilsson, Benjamin Nygren, Alexander Bernhardsson, Anthony Elanga
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Sweden implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Kristoffer Nordfeldt to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Graham Potter demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Sweden excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

# Team Profile: Honduras
- **Manager**: Reinaldo Rueda
- **Key Player**: Luis Palma
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-2-3-1
- **Tactical Identity**: Low block & Wing transition counter-attacks

## Tactical Breakdown
The Honduras national team operates primarily under a 4-2-3-1 shape, focusing on a low block & wing transition counter-attacks style of play. 

### Offensive Phase
During buildup, the manager Reinaldo Rueda instructs the team to look for Luis Palma, who serves as the key reference point in transitions. Wing progression and midfield rotations are crucial to breaking down opposition blocks.

### Defensive Phase
When out of possession, they drop into a compact shape to deny space in between the lines. Depending on their style, they either trigger an aggressive counter-press or establish a disciplined low block.

### Transitions
Quick transitions from defense to attack are prioritized to catch opponents disorganized, leveraging the pace and playmaking ability of Luis Palma.

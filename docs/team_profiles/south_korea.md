# Team Profile: South Korea

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Hong Myungbo
- **Key Players**: Seung-Gyu Kim, Bum-keun Song, Hyeon-woo Jo, Min-Jae Kim, Moon-hwan Kim, Han-bum Lee, Young-woo Seol, Jin-seob Park, Tae-hyeon Kim, Gi-hyuk Lee, Tae-seok Lee, Cho Wi-Je, Jens Castrop, In-beom Hwang, Dong-gyeong Lee, Jae-sung Lee, Jin-Gyu Kim, Seung-Ho Paik, Kang-in Lee, Joon-ho Bae, Hwang Heechan, Heung-min Son, Hyun-jun Yang, Gue-Sung Cho, Hyun-Gyu Oh, Ji-sung Eom
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
South Korea implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Seung-Gyu Kim to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Hong Myungbo demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, South Korea excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

# Team Profile: South Korea

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Hong Myung-bo
- **Key Players**: Son Heung-min, Kim Min-jae, Lee Kang-in, Hwang Hee-chan
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-4-2
- **Tactical Identity**: High-energy transition team, relying heavily on European-based superstars for end product.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
South Korea implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-4-2 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Son Heung-min to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Hong Myung-bo demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, South Korea excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-4-2 structure.

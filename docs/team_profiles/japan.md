# Team Profile: Japan

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Hajime Moriyasu
- **Key Players**: Keisuke Ōsako, Zion Suzuki, Tomoki Hayakawa, Yūto Nagatomo, Takehiro Tomiyasu, Hiroki Ito, Yukinari Sugawara, Ko Itakura, Shogo Taniguchi, Ayumu Seko, Tsuyoshi Watanabe, Junnosuke Suzuki, Wataru Endō, Daichi Kamada, Takefusa Kubo, Keito Nakamura, Ao Tanaka, Kaishu Sano, Yuito Suzuki, Ritsu Doan, Daizen Maeda, Koki Ogawa, Junya Ito, Ayase Ueda, Kento Shiogai, Keisuke Goto
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Japan implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Keisuke Ōsako to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Hajime Moriyasu demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Japan excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

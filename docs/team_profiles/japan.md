# Team Profile: Japan

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Hajime Moriyasu
- **Key Players**: Takefusa Kubo, Wataru Endo, Kaoru Mitoma, Takehiro Tomiyasu
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-2-3-1
- **Tactical Identity**: Incredibly disciplined collective pressing paired with elite dribblers on the flanks.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Japan implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-2-3-1 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Takefusa Kubo to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a aggressive high block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Hajime Moriyasu demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Japan excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-2-3-1 structure.

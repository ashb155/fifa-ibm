# Team Profile: Brazil

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Carlo Ancelotti
- **Key Players**: Weverton, Alisson Becker, Ederson, Roger Ibañez, Bremer, Alex Sandro, Marquinhos, Douglas Santos, Danilo Luiz da Silva, Léo Pereira, Gabriel Magalhães, Wesley, Lucas Paquetá, Bruno Guimarães, Casemiro, Fabinho, Danilo dos Santos de Oliveira, Vinicius Junior, Neymar, Matheus Cunha, Raphinha, Martinelli, Thiago, Luiz Henrique, Endrick, Rayan
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Brazil implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Weverton to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Carlo Ancelotti demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Brazil excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

# Team Profile: Canada

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Jesse Marsch
- **Key Players**: Maxime Crépeau, Dayne St. Clair, Owen Goodman, Alphonso Davies, Richie Laryea, Alfie Jones, Joel Waterman, Derek Cornelius, Alistair Johnston, Moise Bombito, Luc De Fougerolles, Jonathan Osorio, Stephen Eustáquio, Mathieu Choinière, Tajon Buchanan, Marcelo Flores, Nathan-Dylan Saliba, Ismael Kone, Ali Ahmed, Niko Sigur, Jonathan David, Cyle Larin, Liam Millar, Jacob Shaffelburg, Tani Oluwaseyi, Promise Akinpelu
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Canada implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Maxime Crépeau to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Jesse Marsch demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Canada excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

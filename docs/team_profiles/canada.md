# Team Profile: Canada

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Jesse Marsch
- **Key Players**: Alphonso Davies, Jonathan David, Stephen Eustáquio, Ismaël Koné
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-2-2-2
- **Tactical Identity**: High-intensity pressing and quick vertical transitions.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Canada implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-2-2-2 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Alphonso Davies to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a aggressive high block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Jesse Marsch demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Canada excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-2-2-2 structure.

# Team Profile: Argentina

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Lionel Scaloni
- **Key Players**: Géronimo Rulli, Emiliano Martínez, Juan Musso, Nicolás Otamendi, Nicolás Tagliafico, Gonzalo Montiel, Christian Romero, Facundo Medina, Lisandro Martínez, Nahuel Molina, Leonardo Balerdi, Valentin Barco, Rodrigo de Paul, Leandro Paredes, Giovani Lo Celso, Exequiel Palacios, Alexis Mac Allister, Thiago Almada, Enzo Fernández, Nico Paz, Lionel Messi, Lautaro Martínez, Nicolás González, Julián Álvarez, José Manuel López, Giuliano Simeone
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Argentina implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Géronimo Rulli to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Lionel Scaloni demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Argentina excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

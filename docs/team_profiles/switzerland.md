# Team Profile: Switzerland

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Murat Yakin
- **Key Players**: Granit Xhaka, Manuel Akanji, Xherdan Shaqiri, Yann Sommer
- **Preferred Formation**: 3-4-2-1
- **Tactical Identity**: Disciplined pressing structure, making them incredibly difficult to break down.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Switzerland implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 3-4-2-1 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Granit Xhaka to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a aggressive high block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Murat Yakin demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Switzerland excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 3-4-2-1 structure.

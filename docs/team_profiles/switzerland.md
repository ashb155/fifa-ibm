# Team Profile: Switzerland

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Murat Yakin
- **Key Players**: Gregor Kobel, Yvon Mvogo, Marvin Keller, Manuel Akanji, Ricardo Rodriguez, Silvan Widmer, Nico Elvedi, Eray Cömert, Miro Muheim, Aurele Amenda, Luca Jaquez, Remo Freuler, Granit Xhaka, Denis Zakaria, Michel Aebischer, Djibril Sow, Christian Fassnacht, Ruben Vargas, Ardon Jasari, Fabian Rieder, Johan Manzambi, Breel Embolo, Noah Okafor, Cédric Itten, Dan Ndoye, Zeki Amdouni
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Switzerland implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Gregor Kobel to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Murat Yakin demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Switzerland excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

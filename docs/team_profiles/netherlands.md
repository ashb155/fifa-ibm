# Team Profile: Netherlands

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Ronald Koeman
- **Key Players**: Mark Flekken, Bart Verbruggen, Robin Roefs, Denzel Dumfries, Virgil van Dijk, Nathan Aké, Jan Paul van Hecke, Jurrien Timber, Mickey van de Ven, Jorrel Hato, Marten de Roon, Donyell Malen, Frenkie de Jong, Noa Lang, Guus Til, Teun Koopmeiners, Tijjani Reijnders, Ryan Gravenberch, Mats Wieffer, Quinten Timber, Crysencio Summerville, Cody Gakpo, Justin Kluivert, Wout Weghorst, Memphis Depay, Brian Brobbey
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Netherlands implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Mark Flekken to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Ronald Koeman demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Netherlands excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

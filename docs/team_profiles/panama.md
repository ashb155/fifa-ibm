# Team Profile: Panama

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Thomas Christiansen
- **Key Players**: Luis Mejía, Orlando Mosquera, Cesar Samudio, Roderick Miller, Fidel Escobar, Éric Davis, Andrés Andrade, César Blackman, Jiovany Ramos, José Cordoba, Michael Murillo, Edgardo Fariña, Jorge Gutiérrez, Adalberto Carrasquilla, José Luis Rodríguez, Cecilio Waterman, Édgar Bárcenas, Aníbal Godoy, Alberto Quintero, Carlos Harvey, César Yanis, Christian Martinez, José Fajardo, Ismael Díaz, Tomas Rodriguez, Azarias Londoño
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Panama implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Luis Mejía to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Thomas Christiansen demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Panama excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

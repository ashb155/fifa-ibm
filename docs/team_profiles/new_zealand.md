# Team Profile: New Zealand

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Darren Bazeley
- **Key Players**: Max Crocombe, Michael Woud, Alex Paulsen, Liberato Cacace, Tommy Smith, Michael Boxall, Francis De Vries, Callan Elliot, Tim Payne, Nando Pijnaker, Finn Surman, Tyler Bindon, Alex Rufer, Sarpreet Singh, Ryan Thomas, Callum McCowatt, Joe Bell, Marko Stamenic, Ben Old, Kosta Barbarouses, Chris Wood, Ben Waine, Elijah Just, Matthew Garbett, Lachlan Bayliss, Jesse Randall
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
New Zealand implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Max Crocombe to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Darren Bazeley demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, New Zealand excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

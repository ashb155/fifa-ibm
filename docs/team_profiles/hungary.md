# Team Profile: Hungary
- **Manager**: Marco Rossi
- **Key Player**: Dominik Szoboszlai
- **Preferred Formation**: 3-4-2-1
- **Tactical Identity**: Compact Mid-Block & Inside-Forward Runs

## Tactical Breakdown
The Hungary national team operates primarily under a 3-4-2-1 shape, focusing on a compact mid-block & inside-forward runs style of play. 

### Offensive Phase
During buildup, the manager Marco Rossi instructs the team to look for Dominik Szoboszlai, who serves as the key reference point in transitions. Wing progression and midfield rotations are crucial to breaking down opposition blocks.

### Defensive Phase
When out of possession, they drop into a compact shape to deny space in between the lines. Depending on their style, they either trigger an aggressive counter-press or establish a disciplined low block.

### Transitions
Quick transitions from defense to attack are prioritized to catch opponents disorganized, leveraging the pace and playmaking ability of Dominik Szoboszlai.

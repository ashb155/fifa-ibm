# Team Profile: Germany

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Julian Nagelsmann
- **Key Players**: Jamal Musiala, Florian Wirtz, Joshua Kimmich, İlkay Gündoğan
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-2-3-1
- **Tactical Identity**: Gegenpressing system with dual '10s' creating havoc between the lines.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Germany implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-2-3-1 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Jamal Musiala to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a aggressive high block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Julian Nagelsmann demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Germany excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-2-3-1 structure.

# Team Profile: Germany

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Julian Nagelsmann
- **Key Players**: Oliver Baumann, Manuel Neuer, Alexander Nübel, Jonathan Tah, Waldemar Anton, Antonio Rüdiger, David Raum, Nico Schlotterbeck, Malick Thiaw, Nathaniel Brown, Nadiem Amiri, Joshua Kimmich, Leon Goretzka, Pascal Groß, Florian Wirtz, Felix Kalu Nmecha, Angelo Stiller, Jamal Musiala, Aleksandar Pavlović, Assan Ouédraogo, Kai Havertz, Leroy Sané, Deniz Undav, Jamie Leweling, Nick Woltemade, Maximilian Beier
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Germany implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Oliver Baumann to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Julian Nagelsmann demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Germany excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

# Team Profile: Czech Republic

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Miroslav Koubek
- **Key Players**: Jindřich Staněk, Matej Kovar, Lukáš Horníček, Jaroslav Zelený, Vladimir Coufal, Tomáš Holeš, David Zima, David Jurásek, Robin Hranac, Štěpán Chaloupek, Ladislav Krejčí, Vladimír Darida, Tomáš Souček, David Douděra, Michal Sadílek, Lukáš Provod, Pavel Šulc, Lukáš Červ, Denis Višinský, Alexandr Sojka, Hugo Sochůrek, Patrik Schick, Tomáš Chorý, Jan Kuchta, Adam Hložek, Mojmír Chytil
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Czech Republic implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Jindřich Staněk to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Miroslav Koubek demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Czech Republic excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

# Team Profile: Iraq

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Jesús Casas
- **Key Players**: Aymen Hussein, Ali Jasim, Amir Al-Ammari, Zidane Iqbal
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-2-3-1
- **Tactical Identity**: Technical and passionate, focusing on sustained possession in the opposition half.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Iraq implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-2-3-1 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Aymen Hussein to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Jesús Casas demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Iraq excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-2-3-1 structure.

# Team Profile: Iraq

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Graham Arnold
- **Key Players**: Jalal Hassan, Fahad Talib, Ahmed Basil, Hussein Ali, Merchas Doski, Rebin Sulaka, Mustafa Saadoun, Ahmed Yahya, Frans Putros, Zaid Tahseen, Manaf Younis, Akam Hashem, Amir Al Ammari, Kevin Yakob, Aimar Sher, Ibrahim Bayesh, Youssef Amyn, Ahmed Qasem, Zidane Iqbal, Zaid Ismail, Aymen Hussein, Ali Al-Hamadi, Marko Farji, Ali Jassim El-Aibi, Mohanad Ali, Ali Yousif
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Iraq implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Jalal Hassan to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Graham Arnold demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Iraq excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

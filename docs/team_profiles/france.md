# Team Profile: France

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Didier Deschamps
- **Key Players**: Brice Samba, Mike Maignan, Robin Risser, Theo Hernández, Lucas Digne, Lucas Hernández, Jules Koundé, Dayot Upamecano, Ibrahima Konaté, William Saliba, Maxence Lacroix, Malo Gusto, N'Golo Kanté, Adrien Rabiot, Aurélien Tchouameni, Manu Koné, Rayan Cherki, Maghnes Akliouche, Desire Doue, Warren Zaïre-Emery, Jean-Philippe Mateta, Ousmane Dembélé, Kylian Mbappé, Marcus Thuram-Ulien, Michael Olise, Bradley Barcola
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
France implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Brice Samba to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Didier Deschamps demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, France excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

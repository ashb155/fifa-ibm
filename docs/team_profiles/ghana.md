# Team Profile: Ghana

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Carlos Queiroz
- **Key Players**: Lawrence Ati-Zigi, Joseph Anang, Benjamin Asare, Abdul Rahman Baba, Derrick Luckassen, Gideon Mensah, Abdul Mumin, Jerome Opoku, Alidu Seidu, Marvin Senaya, Jonas Adjei Adjetey, Kojo Peprah Oppong, Thomas Partey, Elisha Owusu, Kwasi Sibo, Augustine Boakye, Caleb Yirenkyi, Antoine Semenyo, Brandon Thomas-Asante, Jordan Ayew, Iñaki Williams, Kamal Deen Sulemana, Ernest Nuamah, Abdul Issahaku, Christopher Bonsu Baah, Prince Adu
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Ghana implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Lawrence Ati-Zigi to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Carlos Queiroz demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Ghana excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

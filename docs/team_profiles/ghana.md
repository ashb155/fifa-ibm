# Team Profile: Ghana

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Otto Addo
- **Key Players**: Mohammed Kudus, Thomas Partey, Inaki Williams, Jordan Ayew
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-2-3-1
- **Tactical Identity**: Dynamic and unpredictable, relying heavily on the individual brilliance of their attacking midfielders.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Ghana implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-2-3-1 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Mohammed Kudus to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Otto Addo demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Ghana excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-2-3-1 structure.

# Team Profile: Austria

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Ralf Rangnick
- **Key Players**: Alexander Schlager, Patrick Pentz, Florian Wiegele, David Alaba, Stefan Posch, Philipp Mwene, Kevin Danso, Marco Friedl, Philipp Lienhart, Michael Svoboda, Alexander Prass, David Affengruber, Florian Grillitsch, Alessandro Schöpf, Konrad Laimer, Marcel Sabitzer, Xaver Schlager, Romano Schmid, Christoph Baumgartner, Nicolas  Seiwald, Patrick Wimmer, Carney Chukwuemeka, Paul Wanner, Michael Gregoritsch, Marko Arnautovic, Saša Kalajdžić
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Austria implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Alexander Schlager to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Ralf Rangnick demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Austria excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

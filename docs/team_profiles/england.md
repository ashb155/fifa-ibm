# Team Profile: England

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Thomas Tuchel
- **Key Players**: Harry Kane, Jude Bellingham, Phil Foden, Declan Rice
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-2-3-1
- **Tactical Identity**: Controlled possession, exploiting half-spaces and utilizing a double pivot for stability.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
England implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-2-3-1 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Harry Kane to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Thomas Tuchel demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, England excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-2-3-1 structure.

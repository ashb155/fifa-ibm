# Team Profile: England

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Thomas Tuchel
- **Key Players**: Jordan Pickford, Dean Henderson, James Trafford, John Stones, Dan Burn, Ezri Konsa, Reece James, Djed Spence, Marc Guéhi, Valentino Livramento, Jarell Quansah, Nico O'Reilly, Jordan Henderson, Eberechi Eze, Anthony Gordon, Declan Rice, Morgan Rogers, Elliot Anderson, Jude Bellingham, Kobbie Mainoo, Marcus Rashford, Ollie Watkins, Ivan Toney, Harry Kane, Bukayo Saka, Noni Madueke
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
England implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Jordan Pickford to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Thomas Tuchel demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, England excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

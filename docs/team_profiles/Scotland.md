# Team Profile: Scotland

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Steve Clarke
- **Key Players**: Angus Gunn, Craig Gordon, Liam Kelly, Grant Hanley, Dominic Hyam, Andrew Robertson, Kieran Tierney, Jack Hendry, Anthony Ralston, John Souttar, Scott McKenna, Aaron Hickey, Nathan Patterson, Scott McTominay, Kenny McLean, Ryan Christie, Lewis Ferguson, John McGinn, Ben Doak, Findlay Curtis, Tyler Fletcher, Che Adams, George Hirst, Ross Stewart, Lyndon Dykes, Lawrence Shankland
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Scotland implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Angus Gunn to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Steve Clarke demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Scotland excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

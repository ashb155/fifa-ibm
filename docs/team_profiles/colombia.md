# Team Profile: Colombia

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Néstor Lorenzo
- **Key Players**: Camilo Vargas, David Ospina, Álvaro Montero, Santiago Arias, Johan Mojica, Yerry Mina, Davinson Sánchez, Deiver Machado, Daniel Muñoz, Jhon Lucumí, Willer Ditta, James Rodríguez, Jefferson Lerma, Jhon Arias, Jorge Carrascal, Kevin Castaño, Juan Quintero, Jaminton Campaz, Richard Rios, Juan Portilla, Gustavo Puerta, Jhon Córdoba, Luis Díaz, Luis Suárez, Cucho Hernández, Carlos Gómez
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Colombia implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Camilo Vargas to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Néstor Lorenzo demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Colombia excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

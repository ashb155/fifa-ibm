# Team Profile: Colombia

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Néstor Lorenzo
- **Key Players**: Luis Díaz, James Rodríguez, Jefferson Lerma, Davinson Sánchez
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-2-3-1
- **Tactical Identity**: Technical buildup through the midfield with explosiveness in the final third.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Colombia implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-2-3-1 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Luis Díaz to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Néstor Lorenzo demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Colombia excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-2-3-1 structure.

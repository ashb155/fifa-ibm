# Team Profile: Belgium

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Rudi Garcia
- **Key Players**: Thibaut Courtois, Senne Lammens, Mike Penders, Timothy Castagne, Thomas Meunier, Brandon Mechele, Maxim De Cuyper, Arthur Theate, Koni De Winter, Zeno Debast, Nathan Ngoy, Joaquin Seys, Kevin De Bruyne, Axel Witsel, Youri Tielemans, Dodi Lukebakio, Alexis Saelemaekers, Hans Vanaken, Nicolas Raskin, Jeremy Doku, Amadou Onana, Diego Moreira, Romelu Lukaku, Leandro Trossard, Charles De Ketelaere, Matias Fernandez-Pardo
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Belgium implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Thibaut Courtois to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Rudi Garcia demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Belgium excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

# Team Profile: United States

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Mauricio Pochettino
- **Key Players**: Matt Turner, Matt Freese, Chris Brady, Antonee Robinson, Tim Ream, Miles Robinson, Auston Trusty, Mark McKenzie, Joseph Scally, Sergiño Dest, Chris Richards, Alex Freeman, Weston McKennie, Alejandro Zendejas, Cristian Roldan, Tyler Adams, Brenden Aaronson, Gio Reyna, Sebastian Berhalter, Malik Tillman, Christian Pulisic, Tim Weah, Haji Wright, Ricardo Pepi, Folarin Balogun, Max Arfsten
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
United States implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Matt Turner to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Mauricio Pochettino demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, United States excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.

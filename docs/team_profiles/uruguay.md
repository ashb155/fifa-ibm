# Team Profile: Uruguay

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Marcelo Bielsa
- **Key Players**: Federico Valverde, Darwin Núñez, Ronald Araújo, Manuel Ugarte
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-1-4-1
- **Tactical Identity**: Relentless man-to-man pressing and extremely fast offensive transitions.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Uruguay implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-1-4-1 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Federico Valverde to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a aggressive high block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Marcelo Bielsa demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Uruguay excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-1-4-1 structure.

# Team Profile: Poland

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Michał Probierz
- **Key Players**: Robert Lewandowski, Piotr Zieliński, Wojciech Szczęsny, Jakub Kiwior
- **Preferred Formation**: 3-5-2
- **Tactical Identity**: Deep defending attempting to funnel everything through their world-class striker on the counter.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Poland implements a vertical and direct approach. Operating primarily in a 3-5-2 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Robert Lewandowski to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Michał Probierz demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Poland excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 3-5-2 structure.

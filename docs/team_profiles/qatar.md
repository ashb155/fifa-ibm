# Team Profile: Qatar

## General Overview
- **Manager**: Julen Lopetegui
- **Key Players**: Salah Zakaria, Meshaal Barsham, Mahmoud Abunada, Boualem Khoukhi, Jassem Gaber Abdulsallam, Homam Ahmed, Ró-Ró, Lucas Mendes, Sultan Al Brake, Hashemi Al Hussain, Issa Laye, Ayoub Al-Oui, Assim Madibo, Abdulaziz Hatem, Karim Boudiaf, Ahmed Fathy, Yusuf Abdurisag, Ahmed Al Ganehi, Tahsin Jamshid, Mohamed Al-Mannai, Akram Afif, Almoez Ali, Mohammed Muntari, Hassan Al Haydos, Ahmed Alaa Eldin, Edmílson
- **Preferred Formation**: 4-3-3
- **Tactical Identity**: Balanced tactical approach.

## Tactical Breakdown
### Attacking Phase
Qatar implements a patient build-up and positional play approach. Operating primarily in a 4-3-3 shape, the midfield looks to establish dominance early. When progressing the ball, they look for Salah Zakaria to act as the primary catalyst.

### Defensive Structure
Defensively, they utilize a disciplined mid/low block. The defensive line coordinates closely with the midfield pivot to deny space between the lines. Julen Lopetegui demands extreme discipline out of possession.

### Transitions
In transition moments, Qatar excels at immediate reactions. Offensively, they target the half-spaces instantly upon regaining the ball. Defensively, they attempt to cut off passing lanes and delay the opposition to reform their 4-3-3 structure.
